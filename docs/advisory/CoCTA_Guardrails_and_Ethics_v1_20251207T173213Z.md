## 1. Summary

CoCivium and the CoSuite explicitly reject manipulative nudging and dark patterns.

Our internal "CoCTA" (call to action) mechanisms are designed as:
- gentle prompts, not coercive nags,
- rate-limited and role-shaped,
- piloted in small scopes before any broader use,
- governed by clear ethics and validator roles.

## 3. External Story (CoPolitic-facing)

When speaking to policymakers, investors, partners and CoCivites, we can say:

- The CoSuite prefers **informed autonomy** over behavioral manipulation.
- Guardrails and prompts are:
  - transparent and auditable,
  - tuned to the responsibilities of each role,
  - designed to be easy to mute, tune or override by stewards.

- Any escalation of prompts is:
  - justified by severity (e.g. clear harm or safety risk),
  - reviewed by stewardship roles (Co1/Co4),
  - documented in public or semi-public governance artefacts.
