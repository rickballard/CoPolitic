Orientation

The perspective plane: a simple map for a complicated century

We needed a picture that an exhausted minister, a restless billionaire and a frightened student could all look at together and say, "I see where we are, and I see what moving would mean." The perspective plane is that picture.

The axes

Two questions that quietly decide almost everything

The plane has only two axes. They are blunt on purpose. That makes them hard to game and easy to remember in the middle of real work.

  • Horizontal axis – Who really holds the power here?
    On the left, power is concentrated: a small set of actors, platforms or states make the real decisions. On the right, power is distributed: communities, workers and diverse institutions have meaningful say.
  • Vertical axis – What is decision making aligned to?
    At the bottom, decisions are aligned mostly to short term advantage and narrative control. At the top, they are aligned more to shared truth, long term wellbeing and real feedback from those affected.

Any institution, policy, platform, campaign or AI system can be plotted somewhere on this plane. That dot will not stay still. It will drift under market pressure, political pressure and model pressure unless someone steers it.
