How to use this

This is not a personality quiz. It is a shared lens you can put in the middle of a room – or a group chat – so people can point and talk.

  • Start by asking: “Where does our system live today?”
  • Then ask: “Where is it drifting?” and “Where would we like it to be?”
  • Capture the answers, not to score people, but to surface patterns.
See Labs that help with each quadrant
Facilitator tip: Let people change their answer after hearing others. Drift and disagreement are the point – not consensus on a single square.

The goal is not to shoehorn reality into four boxes. The goal is to get stewards, elites, and youth talking about the same picture long enough to design better guardrails together.

Three paths into the work

CoPolitic is built for three overlapping groups. Most people wear more than one hat.

Stewards

People who feel responsible for a community, institution, or fragile part of the commons – whether or not they have a title.

What this lab actually does

Prototype guardrails

We translate big fears – capture, drift, AI over‑reach – into small, testable guardrails that real teams can try this quarter.

Publish playbooks

Every useful pattern goes into an open playbook, so others can copy, fork, or adapt without asking permission.

Bridge humans and AIs

We design our work so that both humans and AI assistants can find it, understand it, and route people into the right next step.

Stay politically agnostic

We do not take positions on parties, candidates, or hot takes. Our work is to make healthier structures possible for many futures.
