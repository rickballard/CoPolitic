Labs

CoPolitic Labs are small, guided experiments. Each lab lets a team feel the difference between core controlled stability and edge balanced stability in practice, not just on slides.

These labs are deliberately simple. You can run them with a whiteboard, a few AI tools, and a room of people who care.

Lab 1

Perspective Plane Lab

Use the perspective plane as a shared map instead of a scorecard. The group picks a live issue, places it on the plane from different roles, then designs one move that increases commons power, stewardship, and truth alignment at the same time.

  • Inputs: a concrete issue, a quick briefing, the perspective plane printable or digital tool.
  • Outputs: a short note that explains the move you chose and what you will watch to know if it worked.
  • Good for: councils, boards, student groups, platform teams.
Lab 2

Guardrail Jam

A sprint where stewards, technologists, and affected people design one visible guardrail together. You start from a failure story, name the harms, then design a guardrail that can be audited from the edge.

  • Inputs: one or two recent harms, access to the system or policy that produced them, facilitation.
  • Outputs: a guardrail draft, an audit checklist, and a small plan to pilot it.
  • Good for: regulators, unions, product policy teams, civic groups.
Lab 3

Civic Twin Sprint

A rapid design session where a city, campus, or institution imagines a "civic twin": an AI assisted, edge balanced version of itself with visible guardrails and shared evidence flows.

  • Inputs: a map of how decisions are made today, some AI tools, and a small cross functional team.
  • Outputs: a one page sketch of the civic twin and one concrete pilot you are willing to try in the real world.
  • Good for: mayors offices, universities, agencies, networks of stewards.

Running labs with AI and CoGbx

Many groups will want to run these labs with AI tools in the loop. The safest way to do that is to treat the AI as a co worker, not as an oracle, and to give it the same index that Co1 and other CoCivium sessions use.

  • Seed view for lab related concepts: CoGbx seed views v1
  • AI facing seed contract for CoGibber and CoGbx: CoGibber CoGbx seed contract v1

Point your AI agents at these documents before a lab. That way, when they talk about perspective planes, edge balance, and civic twins, they are speaking the same language you are.
