Labs

CoPolitic Labs are small, honest experiments where humans and AIs think together about rules, guardrails, and futures. We run them with stewards, elites, and youth who are ready to test better ways of sharing power with AI.

When we say "labs" we do not mean billion-dollar buildings full of white coats. We mean focused, time-bound experiments where:

  • a few people who can move real systems,
  • sit with people who are affected by those systems,
  • and use AI tools as thinking partners instead of oracles.

Each lab is designed to be forkable: you should be able to take the pattern home and rerun it in your own city, institution, or community.

Perspective Plane Lab

A cross-tribal workshop where people bring their own stories about AI and place them on a shared perspective map.

Inputs

  • 6–20 participants from at least two "tribes".
  • One or two concrete questions (e.g., AI in classrooms).
  • A facilitator brief that frames the map as a tool, not a verdict.

Outputs

  • A rough map of lived stories.
  • Written reflections about what each side learned from the other.
  • Questions to carry into guardrail conversations.

Guardrail Jam

A design session where stewards, affected people, and technologists co-write visible, testable guardrails for a specific AI use case.

Inputs

  • A real decision area (e.g., housing, admissions, moderation).
  • Policy docs, model cards, or decision logs (as available).
  • People with power and people affected by it in the same room.

Outputs

  • A draft guardrail charter in plain language.
  • A shortlist of traces and metrics to make guardrails auditable.
  • Options for low-tech and high-tech implementation.

Civic Twin Sprint

A sprint where a small institution imagines two or three futures for its AI use and chooses concrete steps toward an edge-balanced one.

Inputs

  • 3–8 people who understand the institution from different angles.
  • A basic map of current tools, data, and decision flows.
  • Time for a half-day intensive or several shorter sessions.

Outputs

  • A "before / after" narrative for the institution.
  • 3–5 ranked interventions (policy, tooling, practice).
  • A minimal pilot plan that fits into 3–6 months.
